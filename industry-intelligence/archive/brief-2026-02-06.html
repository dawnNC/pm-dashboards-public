Regulatory Compliance Driving Software Refresh Cycles 6 months
HTI-1 (Health IT Interoperability) compliance deadlines are creating urgent software modernization requirements for senior living operators, particularly around USCDI v3 certification. This regulatory push is converting compliance pressure into immediate software purchasing decisions independent of operator financial health or strategic priorities.
Evidence: 1 supporting
What would disprove this:
  • Regulatory deadlines are extended or enforcement is delayed beyond Q2 2026
  • Senior living operators demonstrate <20% software refresh/replacement activity attributed to HTI-1 by mid-2026

AI Adoption Hype-Reality Gap Persists in Senior Living 12 months
Despite industry reports emphasizing AI's strategic importance for senior living (automated documentation, care prediction), actual adoption remains low with over 60% of organizations citing workforce challenges as barriers rather than AI implementation. The gap between vendor messaging and operator readiness is widening, not narrowing.
Evidence: 4 supporting
What would disprove this:
  • By Q3 2026, >40% of senior living operators report active AI-powered care automation deployments (not pilots)
  • AI adoption accelerates faster than workforce hiring/retention improvements, contradicting the stated constraint

Workforce Shortage Becoming Primary Software Buying Driver 12 months
Recruitment and retention crises (cited by 60%+ of operators) are shifting software purchasing priorities away from nice-to-have analytics or resident engagement tools toward labor-replacement and workflow-efficiency solutions. Vendors solving for understaffed operations (care automation, intelligent alerting) are gaining traction over feature-rich platforms.
Evidence: 3 supporting
What would disprove this:
  • Senior living operators' software purchasing shifts toward non-workforce-related categories (e.g., resident engagement, wellness) in H2 2026
  • Workforce recruitment/retention stress improves measurably, reducing urgency for labor-replacement software

Operators are demanding single-pane visibility across care execution and clinical documentation rather than point solutions.

API/Integration Standards (MCP) Emerging as Platform Expectation 24+ months
While generic SaaS is moving toward Model Context Protocol (MCP) endpoints as standard, senior living SaaS vendors will face similar pressure to ship integration capabilities by default, similar to REST API adoption in the 2010s. Vendors without robust API/integration roadmaps will face competitive disadvantage.
Evidence: 1 supporting
What would disprove this:
  • By end of 2026, <30% of new senior living SaaS launches advertise native MCP or equivalent API-first architecture
  • Existing senior living SaaS platforms remain fragmented; integration still requires manual ETL or custom middleware in majority of deployments

Large operators like Brookdale are downsizing portfolios and pursuing leaner organizational models. As operators close communities and consolidate facilities, they reduce overall software licensing seats and purchasing power, creating a near-term headwind for vendor growth even as they modernize remaining properties.
Evidence: 1 supporting
What would disprove this:

Supply-Demand Mismatch Creating Urgency for Operational Efficiency Software 24+ months
With the industry unable to build supply fast enough to meet boomer demand surge (would require 2x historical development pace for 20 years), operators are investing in software to maximize occupancy, reduce operational waste, and improve efficiency from existing capacity rather than build new facilities. Operational intelligence and yield management software will see increased demand.
Evidence: 4 supporting
What would disprove this:
  • Senior living development activity restarts at historically normal levels by end of 2026, reducing urgency for efficiency software
  • Operators report software spending on operational efficiency declining in 2026 budgets despite supply constraints
